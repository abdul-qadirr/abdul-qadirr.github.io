Monsoon cropland mapping in India

Monitoring monsoon crops are challenging due to cloud cover during critical crop growth stages, landscape heterogeneity and small field sizes. The objective of this research is to develop a machine learning based model for mapping monsoon crops by fusing optical and SAR satellite data at high-resolution (10m) appropriate for different agroecological regions (AER) and small field sizes. You can read more about the research in this publication.

Monitoring forest degradation in South Asia using multi source satellite data

The objective of this research is to develop a machine learning based classification model by combining SAR and optical data to capture forest degradation signals in two protected sites in India and Sri Lanka. The classification model was able to generate accuracy of >90% to identify non-intact forest within the protected areas. This assessment contributed towards Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) indicator of 15.1.1 and 15.1.2. You can read more about the research in this publication.

Robot Co-Labourers for Intelligent Farming

Research project, April, 2019 - Ongoing

This is an ongoing project at King's College London at the interactionLAb. We are focused on studying methods to enable collaborations between human and robots in agricultural settings. Among the multiple research outputs, our studies show how to improve human opinion towards robot ro-workers. For more information about these topics you can take a look to our publications. Within this project, I am particularly interested in adapting computer vision algorithms to make them trustworthy.
